Introduction

With the improvement of the economy and living standards, transportation has seen a rise to its demand. This has been felt in the railway industry of high-speed trains where they have constantly continued to improve and meet demands. High speed trains not only provide fast and reliable journey through a country but connects economic hubs, boosting the economy. Not only is it key to boosting economic growth, it also eco-friendly with low carbon emissions and more energy efficient which in turn has received wide praise and become a staple mode of travel in countries such as China and Japan. This influence has also been felt in Europe where a survey carried out by CER revealed that “3 in 4 EU citizens would take a high-speed train instead of a plane when travelling across Europe for short and medium distances” [1]. To meet demand, high-speed trains continuously operate at high speeds well above 200km/h. The weight of a high-speed train can cause line loss, energy consumption, and cause load on the brake system [2]. They are the one of the biggest achievements in the train industry. It provides a fast and reliable method of transportation for long distance travel between cities. Its efficiency not only extends to this point but also encompasses sustainability in transportation. This article focuses on the applications of lightweight materials Magnesium and Aluminium alloys, in the design of high-speed trains.
